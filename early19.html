1911 Benz 50hp Victoria

The engine was mounted on a 127-in.-wheelbase frame and weighed 2,640 pounds before coachwork was added.It had a dual T-head design with two camshafts, one on the induction side, another on the exhaust side; a dual ignition, with Bosch high tension magneto and supplementary battery/coil, has separate spark plugs and switches for each system.The 50hp Benz offered here is reportedly the sole extant survivor of this rare chassis.it still bears the brass identification plate noting that it was manufactured "expressly for Benz Auto Import Co. of America, 1599 Broadway, New York City, For American Roads."

1905 Cadillac Model E

In 1905, Cadillac launched its new Model E, which was a conventional “modern automobile” with a front-mounted, 30-horsepower, single-cylinder engine on a 100-inch wheelbase chassis the actual engine was not under the hood; it was still under the seat. The brass-trimmed Model E enjoyed sprightly performance.“Even after a year’s service, it is not unusual for a Cadillac motor, when given the proper care, to develop 20 percent more power than originally rated. Ample reserve energy enables the Cadillac to go anywhere.”

1914 Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost

In 1913 the Colonial model Silver Ghost chassis was developed for sale in India.Its mascot name was “The Spirit of Ecstasy”.A top speed of 80+ mph was possible in a Ghost like this one. In 1914 the New York price for a Silver Ghost chassis was $7,000. Said Rolls-Royce: “The quality will remain long after the price is forgotten”.
